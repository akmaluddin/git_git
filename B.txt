As the town's curiosity grew, so did the number of visitors to Alfred's garden. Children would stop by, their eyes wide with wonder, to peek at the rare and beautiful bird. They'd ask questions about her, and Alfred would answer them with a gentle smile, his voice filled with a warmth that hadn't been heard in years. The adults, too, began to visit, not just to see Marigold but to speak with Alfred. They discovered a man who, behind the veil of solitude, had a wealth of knowledge about the natural world and a kindness that had long been hidden.
