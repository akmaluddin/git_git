The task of mending the wing was delicate and painstaking. He worked under the dim light of a single bulb that swung gently overhead, casting elongated shadows across the kitchen floor. Each feather was carefully cleaned and inspected before he set the wing into place. The bird's chirps grew softer as the night drew on, until they were replaced by the quiet rhythm of its breathing. Alfred felt a sense of purpose that had eluded him for a long time. This creature had entrusted its fate to him, and he was determined not to disappoint.
